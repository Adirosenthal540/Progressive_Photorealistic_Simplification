We address specific concerns below.

### Common Suggestions.
 The Concept of "Photorealistic Abstraction" & Scientific Contribution (R2, R4): R4 noted that "photorealistic abstraction" might sound like an oxymoron, and R2 expressed concern that the work might be reduced to "carefully crafting prompts." We strongly agree that prompting alone does not constitute science. Traditional abstraction alters both content and style. In this paper, we strictly isolate and formalize subtractive abstraction the programmed, sequential removal of elements while maintaining the photorealism of the remaining scene. By defining a semantic taxonomy and logical extraction ranking, we aim to teach AI models to understand visual hierarchy similarly to humans. While extreme decluttering may push subjective boundaries (R4), our core contribution is defining a programmable scheme that models can learn and that correlates strongly with human agreement.

### Reviewer 1.
Direct Prompting Baseline: Testing NanoBanana Pro and Gemini 3.0 Pro revealed two critical limitations: 1) End-to-End Generation Failure: Subtractive abstraction of natural scenes as we defined it on section 3 (removing one element in every step) typically requires more than 6 steps. Current models like NanoBanana Pro are constrained to short sequences (4–6 frames) and cannot generate full trajectories. 2) Iterative Prompting Degradation: Step-by-step editing on current image editors rapidly degrades image quality. Without our alignment, masking, blending, and verification steps, the model routinely loses strict photorealism over multiple steps. Our pipeline provides essential guardrails that will extract high quality trajectories.
Shelf-life: While low-level heuristics currently compensate for generative limitations, our core conceptual framework (the taxonomy, Select-Remove-Verify algorithm, and distillation) is model-agnostic. Future foundation models will simply plug into this blueprint, increasing its robustness.
Thank you for catching the chronological oversight. In the revised manuscript, we have corrected this by introducing stroke-based rendering [Hertzmann 1998] as a foundational method first, followed chronologically by the work of DeCarlo and Santella and subsequent real-time filters. We also and removed the repeated lines.

### Reviewer 3.
We are thrilled that you found our core concept to be a clever, original, and timely direction that opens new possibilities for computational photography.

Masking vs. Resynthesis: As you correctly deduced, we want to remove global effects like shadows and reflections. Furthermore, our pipeline lacks an initial object mask because the VLM designer only outputs text (the object name). Thus, the generative editor processes the full image to naturally erase the object and its effects. Only after this removal do we compute a difference mask for smooth blending, which avoids the histogram adjustments and warping issues you mentioned. We will clarify this rationale in the main paper.

Fig. 4 & Bias: The confusion matrics are asymmetric because they are row-normalized. We used the exact same dataset for both matrices, meaning no selection bias exists. We will detail the matrics creation process in the supplementary material.

Tab. 3 & Verification Classifier: As you suspected, the Order Accuracy metric measures only semantic ordering, not visual fidelity. The primary purpose of the verification classifier is to act as a strict visual quality gate, keeping the generated sequence strictly photorealistic. The ablation simply verifies that applying this strict quality filter does not degrade the semantic order accuracy. We will add visual examples of rejected artifacts to visually demonstrate its utility.

Additional Details & Clarifications: We used the exact same prompt for all of our baseline image-to-video models. We will make sure to clarify this in the paper and include the exact prompts in the supplementary material. Additionally, the supplementary material will be updated to include the classifier's training data construction and labeling details, the precise manual filtering criteria from Sec 5.4, and details regarding the raters' backgrounds (all of whom were professional photographers).
